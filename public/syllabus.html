Syllabus

Unit I: Foundation of Computer Graphics (Week 1 - 2)

  • Objectives: Course overview and study computer graphics foundation.
  • Lectures: Course Overview, Geometry Modeling, Appearance Modeling, Ray Tracing.
  • Assignments: Ray Tracing with GLSL.

Unit II: Implicit Neural Representation (Week 3-5)

  • Objectives: Explore the mathematical and algorithmic foundations for implicit neural representations, focusing on the Signed Distance Field (SDF) as a universal shape representation.
  • Lectures: Foundation of Signed Distance Field, SDF Primitives and Operations, SDF Rendering, SDF Representation: from Grid to Neural Network I, SDF Representation: from Grid to Neural Network II.
  • Assignments: (A) SDF and Ray Marching and (B) Neural Implicit Surface.

Unit III: Neural Radiance Field (Week 5-7)

  • Objectives: Explore the mathematical foundations of radiance fields, volume rendering equations, and Neural Radiance Field (NeRF).
  • Lectures: Mathematical Foundation of Radiance Field, Participating Media and Volumetric Rendering, Neural Radiance Field, Accelerating NeRF, Hybrid Representations.
  • Assignment: (A) Volumetric Rendering and (B) NeRF.

Unit IV: Gaussian Splatting (Week 8-10)

  • Objectives: Investigate point-based graphics, differentiable rendering, and its modern evolution into 3D Gaussian Splatting.
  • Lectures: Points, Splats, Gaussian Splatting, Advanced Gaussian Splatting.
  • Assignment: Gaussian Splatting.

Unit V: Physics Simulation (Week 11-13)

  • Objectives: Study physics-based simulation techniques, emphasizing position-based dynamics (PBD) and their applications in AI.
  • Lectures: Mathematical Foundation of Physics Simulation, Particle-based Physics Simulation, Position-based Dynamics, Differentiable Physics Simulation, Neural Physics Simulation.
  • Assignment: Position-based Dynamics Simulation.

Unit VI: Generative Models and World Models (Week 14-15)

  • Objectives: Explore cutting-edge generative graphics models and their practical applications in modern computer graphics.
  • Lectures: Mathematical Foundations of Generative Models, Diffusion Models, Score-based Matching and Flow Matching, Geometry Generation, World Models.
  • Assignment: Diffusion Model.
